Position Sizing & Risk Management

Proper risk management is what separates successful traders from gamblers. Here's the exact approach I used to grow my account consistently.

Risk Per Trade:

  • Never risk more than 1-2% of your account on a single trade
  • For a $5,000 account, this means $50-$100 maximum risk per trade
  • Calculate position size based on stop loss distance

Position Size Formula:

Position Size = (Account Risk in $) / (Stop Loss in Pips × Pip Value)

Example: $5,000 account risking 1% ($50) with 20 pip stop loss on EUR/USD (pip value = $10 per lot)

Position Size = $50 / (20 × $10) = 0.25 lots

Stop Loss Placement:

  • Gold: 30-50 cents (3-5 pips) beyond recent swing high/low
  • Forex: 15-25 pips beyond recent swing high/low
  • Never move stop loss further away to "give the trade room"

Take Profit Strategies

The Beast Strategy uses a tiered take profit approach to maximize gains while securing profits along the way.

Three-Tier Profit Taking:

  1. First Target (25% of position): 1:1 risk-reward (e.g., 20 pips if SL is 20 pips)
  2. Second Target (50% of position): 2:1 risk-reward (e.g., 40 pips)
  3. Third Target (25% of position): Let run with trailing stop (minimum 3:1)

Trailing Stop Technique:

  • After hitting second target, move stop to breakeven
  • Trail stop at 50% of recent candle range
  • For gold, trail by $1 (10 pips) for every $5 (50 pips) move
  • For forex, trail by 10 pips for every 30 pip move

Special Cases:

  • During news events, take full profit at 2:1 and exit
  • If price stalls at round numbers (e.g., 1800 in gold), take partial profit
  • Friday afternoons - close all positions before market close

Common Mistakes to Avoid

After coaching hundreds of traders, these are the most common mistakes that prevent success with this strategy.

Psychological Errors:

  • Overtrading: Taking setups that don't meet all criteria because of FOMO
  • Revenge Trading: Trying to immediately recover losses with bigger positions
  • Moving Stops: Widening stop loss when trade goes against you

Technical Errors:

  • Trading during low liquidity periods (Asian session)
  • Ignoring volume spikes that contradict your bias
  • Entering before full candle closes beyond structure
  • Using too many indicators (this strategy needs none)

Risk Management Errors:

  • Increasing position size after losses ("martingale")
  • Not adjusting position size for different instruments
  • Taking trades with poor risk-reward (less than 1:1)

Economic News Adaptation

News Trading Rules:

  • Retracement Entry: Must be 50-61.8% of initial move with volume confirmation
  • News Scalping: Only on 1-minute chart with tight stops (5-10 pips)

Highest Probability News Events:

  2. FOMC Rate Decisions

  4. ECB Press Conferences

News Filter Settings:
